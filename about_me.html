Mr Murray is currently an educational technology specialist at Riverside High School in Yonkers, NY. He teaches college and career oriented computer literacy courses which focus on students completing project-based lessons to demonstrate mastery. Mr Murray has also supported faculty at Riverside High School in implementing technology into classroom instruction through facilitated workshops on blended learning, Google Apps, and Microsoft Office 365. Before this, he worked at the New York Institute of Technology as a technology trainer and was responsible for instituting major instructional technology initiatives with statewide and national scope.

In 2008, Mr Murray was selected to serve as a corps member and team leader in AmeriCorps National Civilian Community Corps (NCCC). The NCCC is a team-based national service program for 18-24 year olds that requires a full-time commitment of traveling, living, and serving in various communities across the nation. Service areas included environmental work, educational work with at-risk youth, and disaster recovery and response project in the gulf region following Hurricane Katrina, Rita, and Ike.

Throughout his life Mr Murray has been an avid runner. He started running in high school and continued to run competitively at the collegiate level at Mercy College in Dobbs Ferry, NY. Mr Murray continues to run competitively in road races in the Long Island area.
